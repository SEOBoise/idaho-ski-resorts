History of Rotarun Ski Area: Discover the origins and development of this iconic ski area, from its humble beginnings to its current status as a beloved winter destination.

Rotarun Ski Area, nestled in the picturesque Wood River Valley of central Idaho, has a rich and storied history that dates back to its humble beginnings in the early 1940s. Originally established as a small community ski hill, it quickly gained popularity among local residents and outdoor enthusiasts alike. Throughout the decades, the ski area underwent several expansions and improvements, transforming it from a modest ski hill into a beloved winter destination. Today, Rotarun Ski Area stands as a testament to the dedication and passion of the community that has nurtured it over the years.

The origins of Rotarun Ski Area can be traced back to the foresight and determination of a group of local businessmen and ski enthusiasts. Recognizing the need for a recreational outlet during the long winter months, they came together and formed the Blaine County Ski Club. With limited resources but boundless enthusiasm, they constructed a small rope tow that provided access to a handful of gentle slopes. Word quickly spread about this new ski area, attracting more visitors and prompting the club to continually expand and improve the facilities. Today, Rotarun Ski Area boasts a variety of terrain and modern amenities, while still retaining its warm and welcoming community atmosphere.
